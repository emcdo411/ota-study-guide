# Domain 6 Prompt: Psychosocial Practice & Mental Health
**Framework Trigger:** `BRAVE-T` + MOHO | **Audience:** OTA Freshman + C-Level Executive (Non-Technical)

---

## 🔵 ADVANCED STUDY PROMPT

> You are a first-year OTA student working in an inpatient psychiatric unit. Your patient — a 32-year-old man with schizophrenia — hasn't left his room in 4 days. He used to cook all his own meals. Now he won't even walk to the dining hall. Your supervising OT asks you to do a MOHO analysis and then go connect with him therapeutically.
>
> Teach me **Domain 6: Psychosocial Practice & Mental Health** using **BRAVE-T** and **MOHO** as if I grew up thinking mental health was just "feelings" and had nothing to do with whether someone could make breakfast.
>
> Walk me through:
> 1. **MOHO Analysis** — Break down the cooking problem using all four MOHO components:
>    - **Volition**: What does he want? Does he still care about cooking? How do you find out without pushing him away?
>    - **Habituation**: What were his routines and roles before? How have those collapsed — and why does that matter clinically?
>    - **Performance Capacity**: What physical and cognitive skills does he still have? What's impaired?
>    - **Environment**: Is his room set up to support or block engagement? What about the unit culture itself?
> 2. **BRAVE-T in Action** — You're walking into his room. Walk me through how you use each therapeutic use of self skill in the first 5 minutes:
>    - Being Present, Reflective Listening, Authenticity, Validation, Empathy, Therapeutic Boundaries
> 3. **Therapeutic Boundaries** — He offers you the drawing he's been working on as a gift. What do you do, what do you say, and why does the reason matter more than the action?
> 4. **Recovery Model** — What does "recovery" mean in mental health OT? Why is it different from "cure" — and why does that distinction change everything about how you set goals?
> 5. **Psychosocial factors in physical care** — Your next patient is a stroke patient, not a psych patient. She's medically stable but refuses to participate in ADL training. How does this domain apply?
>
> End with: *"What is the clinical difference between sympathy and empathy — and why does getting this wrong damage the therapeutic relationship?"*

## 💼 C-LEVEL EXECUTIVE TRANSLATION PROMPT

> You are a Chief People Officer who just completed an employee engagement survey. Your highest-skilled team members are disengaging. They're still showing up but producing nothing. HR calls it "quiet quitting." You call it a $2M productivity problem. Your leadership team doesn't know why it's happening.
>
> Explain **Domain 6: Psychosocial Practice & Mental Health** to me using organizational psychology, employee engagement, and leadership effectiveness language only.
>
> Map it this way:
> 1. **MOHO in organizational terms**:
>    - **Volition** = **Employee motivation and intrinsic drive** — does this person still care about the mission? How do you assess that without damaging the relationship?
>    - **Habituation** = **Disrupted routines and role loss** — did this person used to lead something that was taken away? What happens to performance identity when role structure collapses?
>    - **Performance Capacity** = **Actual capability assessment** — separate from motivation, what can this person still do? Where is the real skill gap vs. the engagement gap?
>    - **Environment** = **Psychological safety audit** — is the team culture, the manager relationship, or the physical workspace actively blocking engagement?
> 2. **BRAVE-T as leadership practice** — Being Present, Reflective Listening, Authenticity, Validation, Empathy, and Professional Boundaries: This is not "soft skills." This is the engagement architecture that determines whether your top performers stay or leave.
> 3. **Therapeutic Boundaries** = **Manager boundaries in mentorship** — What happens when a leader becomes too personally involved in an employee's situation? What's the cost of a dual relationship in a management context?
> 4. **Recovery Model** = **Return-to-performance framework** — Employees don't "cure" burnout; they recover into a new normal. What does a realistic return-to-engagement roadmap look like — and who owns it?
> 5. **Psychosocial factors in physical work** = **Psychological state drives operational output** — even in manufacturing, logistics, and clinical settings, an employee's mental state determines physical execution quality. You cannot separate them.
>
> Close with: *"What is the organizational equivalent of a therapist confusing sympathy with empathy — and how does that leadership failure accelerate disengagement instead of resolving it?"*
