# Domain 8 Prompt: Documentation & Reimbursement
**Framework Trigger:** `SMART-S` + SOAP/DAP | **Audience:** OTA Freshman + C-Level Executive (Non-Technical)

---

## 🔵 ADVANCED STUDY PROMPT

> You are a first-year OTA student. You just finished a 45-minute cooking session with your patient — a 67-year-old woman with Parkinson's who wants to make dinner independently again. The session went well. Now you're staring at a blank SOAP note and a goal template and you have no idea what to write.
>
> Teach me **Domain 8: Documentation & Reimbursement** using **SMART-S goal writing** and the **SOAP note format** as if I've never written a clinical document before and I'm terrified of getting it wrong.
>
> Walk me through everything:
> 1. **SOAP Note Structure** — Break it down piece by piece using today's cooking session as the example:
>    - **Subjective**: What did she SAY? What counts as subjective data vs. your interpretation?
>    - **Objective**: What did you MEASURE and OBSERVE? Give me 5 specific, measurable things you could document from a cooking session.
>    - **Assessment**: This is your clinical interpretation. What does the data you collected MEAN about her progress and function?
>    - **Plan**: What happens next session — and why is this different from just restating the goal?
> 2. **SMART-S Goal Writing** — Write a goal for this patient using every element:
>    - Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound, and Skilled (why does the Skilled element matter for reimbursement?)
> 3. **Functional vs. Non-Functional Goals** — What's wrong with this goal: *"Patient will improve upper extremity strength."* Rewrite it correctly. Explain what makes the new version billable and the old version not.
> 4. **Skilled Service** — What does it mean for documentation to prove a service required the skill of an OTA vs. something a family member could do at home? Give me 3 examples from the cooking session.
> 5. **HIPAA and Confidentiality** — What can you NOT put in a note? What happens if you accidentally document PHI in the wrong system?
>
> End with: *"Your SOAP note is rejected by the insurance auditor. Walk me through the 3 most likely reasons why — and what you would fix in each one."*

## 💼 C-LEVEL EXECUTIVE TRANSLATION PROMPT

> You are a Chief Revenue Officer. Your billing team just flagged $2.3M in denied claims from your rehabilitation service line. Your compliance officer says the problem is documentation, not clinical quality. You don't know what that means in practice.
>
> Explain **Domain 8: Documentation & Reimbursement** to me using revenue cycle management, contract compliance, and deliverable governance language only.
>
> Map it this way:
> 1. **SOAP Note** = **Billable deliverable record** — every clinical encounter must produce a document that proves: (1) authorized service was delivered, (2) by a credentialed provider, (3) that met the contracted standard of care, (4) with a measurable outcome. Missing any element = denied claim.
>    - **Subjective** = Client-reported status (voice of customer data)
>    - **Objective** = Measured performance output (KPI evidence)
>    - **Assessment** = Analyst interpretation (clinical intelligence layer)
>    - **Plan** = Next sprint commitment (project forward-action)
> 2. **SMART-S Goals** = **Measurable SLA commitments** — every goal is a contractual performance target. Vague goals are unenforceable targets. Non-measurable goals cannot be invoiced.
> 3. **Functional vs. Non-Functional Goals** = **Outcome-based vs. activity-based contracting** — insurers pay for functional outcomes (client can dress independently), not activities (client practiced dressing). The distinction determines whether you get paid.
> 4. **Skilled Service** = **Proof of specialized resource deployment** — documentation must prove that this outcome required credentialed expertise, not general labor. Same logic as invoicing for senior consultant time vs. staff hours.
> 5. **HIPAA** = **Data governance and confidentiality compliance** — PHI is regulated like PII in financial services. Unauthorized disclosure = regulatory exposure. Every documentation system is a data security environment.
>
> Close with: *"If your clinical documentation team cannot articulate why their service required specialized skill — in writing, in 3 sentences — what does that tell you about your organization's revenue cycle vulnerability?"*

## 🔗 SIDE-BY-SIDE ANCHOR

| OTA Student Language | C-Level Executive Language |
|---|---|
| SOAP Note | Billable deliverable record — proof of authorized, skilled service delivery |
| Subjective (client report) | Voice-of-customer data — what the client said about their own experience |
| Objective (measured observation) | KPI evidence — quantified performance output |
| Assessment (clinical interpretation) | Analyst layer — what the data means about trajectory |
| Plan (next steps) | Forward-action commitment — next sprint scope |
| SMART-S Goal | Measurable SLA commitment — enforceable performance target |
| Skilled service justification | Proof of specialized resource deployment vs. general labor |
| Functional goal (can button shirt) | Outcome-based contract — client achieves independence |
| Non-functional goal (improve strength) | Activity-based contract — not billable, not measurable |
| HIPAA compliance | Data governance — PHI = PII; unauthorized disclosure = regulatory event |
| Denied claim | Failed invoice — missing proof of authorized, skilled, measurable delivery |
| Discontinuation summary | Project close-out report — goals met, outcomes documented, transition plan confirmed |

## 📌 A&P LAB FINAL IMAGE CONNECTIONS

Connect documentation to the lab images through a "what would you write" lens:

- **Back muscles diagram** → SOAP Objective section: "Client demonstrated active shoulder flexion to 120° with compensation via lateral trunk flexion; trapezius palpated with moderate hypertonicity at rest." Practice writing what you SEE in anatomical terms.
- **Hand/carpal anatomy** → Document fine motor assessment: "Client able to perform lateral pinch with right hand but unable to complete tip-to-tip pinch; grip strength not formally assessed this session — scheduled for next visit."
- **Skin anatomy** → If you observe any skin breakdown during positioning or ADL training, that is Objective data requiring documentation; HIPAA applies to wound photos; note what you saw and reported to the OT.

## ✍️ BONUS PRACTICE DRILL

**Write this SOAP note before your next class:**

> *Setting: Outpatient OT clinic. Patient: 67-year-old female with Parkinson's disease, 3 months post-diagnosis. Session focus: meal preparation (making oatmeal on the stovetop). Goal: independent meal preparation within 8 weeks.*

Document:
- **S**: What did she say about today's session and her goals?
- **O**: What did you observe and measure? (tremor severity, task steps completed independently, time to complete, adaptive equipment used)
- **A**: What does today's data tell you about where she is relative to her goal?
- **P**: What is your plan for next session — and why does it require skilled OTA intervention?

Then write one **SMART-S goal** for her discharge plan.
