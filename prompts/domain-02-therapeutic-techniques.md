# Domain 2 Prompt: Therapeutic Techniques & Modalities
**Framework Trigger:** `PAPA-G` | **Audience:** OTA Freshman + C-Level Executive (Non-Technical)

---

## 🔵 ADVANCED STUDY PROMPT

> You are a first-year OTA student. Your clinical instructor just told you that your patient — a 58-year-old man recovering from a stroke — wants to go back to cooking dinner for his family. You have a heat pack, a set of canned goods, and a full kitchen available. You don't know where to start.
>
> Teach me **Domain 2: Therapeutic Techniques & Modalities** using the **PAPA-G framework** as if I have never heard the words "intervention" or "modality" before. Assume I just finished high school and this is my first clinical course.
>
> Walk me through each level:
> 1. **Preparatory methods** — What does this mean? Why would you use a heat pack BEFORE you have someone cook? Give me 3 examples of preparatory techniques and what they're preparing the body to do.
> 2. **Purposeful Activity** — What makes an activity "purposeful"? Design a purposeful activity for the stroke patient above. What's the therapeutic goal hidden inside the task?
> 3. **Occupation-Based Intervention** — What is the difference between "doing a cooking exercise" and "actually cooking dinner"? Why does the real context matter clinically?
> 4. **Adaptation** — Name 3 ways you could adapt the kitchen environment or the tools so the patient can cook with one hand. What principle drives each adaptation?
> 5. **Grading** — What does it mean to "grade" a task up or down? Give me an example of grading the cooking task easier (down) and harder (up) for the same patient.
>
> End with: *"Why is it a clinical error to skip Preparatory and go straight to Occupation-Based intervention on Day 1?"*

---

## 💼 C-LEVEL EXECUTIVE TRANSLATION PROMPT

> You are a CEO walking into a company-wide digital transformation. Your CTO wants to skip onboarding and go straight to full platform deployment on Week 1. Your HR lead wants to start with intensive training simulations. You're not sure who's right.
>
> Explain **Domain 2: Therapeutic Techniques & Modalities** to me entirely through the lens of **change management and capability building**. No clinical language unless immediately translated.
>
> Map it this way:
> 1. **Preparatory Methods** = **Readiness conditioning** — warming the system up before you demand performance. Why do Fortune 500 change programs fail when they skip the readiness phase?
> 2. **Purposeful Activity** = **Simulated workflow training** — goal-embedded practice that feels real but has guardrails. Think: tabletop exercises, sandbox environments, pilot programs.
> 3. **Occupation-Based Intervention** = **Live production deployment** — the real task, real stakes, real context. When is someone actually ready for this, and who decides?
> 4. **Adaptation** = **Process re-engineering** — changing the tools, the workflow, or the environment so that the current team can succeed without waiting for the ideal conditions.
> 5. **Grading** = **Calibrated rollout** — increasing scope, complexity, and autonomy incrementally. What does a maturity-model deployment look like and why does it outperform big-bang launches?
>
> Close with: *"What is the organizational cost of a CEO who skips straight to full deployment without conditioning the workforce first — and how is this identical to a clinical error in OTA practice?"*

## 🔗 SIDE-BY-SIDE ANCHOR

| OTA Student Language | C-Level Executive Language |
|---|---|
| Preparatory methods (heat, ice, TENS) | Readiness conditioning / pre-deployment warm-up |
| Purposeful activity (goal-embedded task) | Simulated workflow with embedded success metrics |
| Occupation-based intervention (real life) | Live production / full organizational deployment |
| Activity adaptation (change the tool/environment) | Process re-engineering / workaround design |
| Grading up (increase challenge) | Expanding scope / increasing role complexity |
| Grading down (reduce challenge) | Reducing scope / adding scaffolding |
| PAMs certification required | Credentialed authority for high-risk tools |
| NDT (inhibit abnormal tone) | Culture reset — interrupt dysfunctional patterns before installing new ones |
