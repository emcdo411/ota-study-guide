# Domain 5 Prompt: Clinical Reasoning & Problem-Solving
**Framework Trigger:** `NIPCP` | **Audience:** OTA Freshman + C-Level Executive (Non-Technical)

---

## 🔵 ADVANCED STUDY PROMPT

> You are a first-year OTA student in your second week of clinicals. Your patient — a 45-year-old woman recovering from a traumatic brain injury — was making great progress last week. Today she walks in, sits down, crosses her arms, and says nothing. The old treatment plan suddenly feels useless.
>
> Teach me **Domain 5: Clinical Reasoning & Problem-Solving** using the **NIPCP framework** as if I've only ever solved problems in a straight line — step 1, step 2, step 3. Help me understand why clinical thinking doesn't work that way.
>
> Walk me through each reasoning mode using the silent patient scenario above:
> 1. **Narrative Reasoning** — What is her story? What do you need to know about her life before the injury to understand why she's shut down today? What questions do you ask?
> 2. **Interactive Reasoning** — She's not talking. You're sitting across from her. What do you do in the next 60 seconds? What cues are you reading — body language, silence, affect?
> 3. **Procedural Reasoning** — What's your clinical protocol if a patient refuses to engage? Walk me through the steps: what do you attempt, what do you document, who do you contact?
> 4. **Conditional Reasoning** — If she stays disengaged for 3 more sessions, where does her recovery trajectory go? What's the long-term vision you're protecting by not giving up?
> 5. **Pragmatic Reasoning** — You have 45 minutes, the OT is unavailable until 3pm, and the family is waiting in the lobby. What is actually doable right now, within those real-world constraints?
>
> Then: *"The OTA's job in this scenario is NOT to revise the treatment plan. What IS the OTA's job, exactly — and what happens clinically if they improvise instead?"*

## 💼 C-LEVEL EXECUTIVE TRANSLATION PROMPT

> You are a Chief Strategy Officer. Your highest-performing business unit just went quiet. Q3 numbers dropped. The VP hasn't returned your last two emails. You're three weeks from a board presentation and something is wrong, but you don't know what.
>
> Explain **Domain 5: Clinical Reasoning & Problem-Solving** to me using only strategic leadership, decision-making, and situational intelligence language.
>
> Map it this way:
> 1. **Narrative Reasoning** = **Context intelligence** — before you intervene, you need the backstory. What was this unit's history, culture, and recent stress events? Leaders who skip context misdiagnose constantly.
> 2. **Interactive Reasoning** = **Real-time situational awareness** — reading the room, sensing resistance, adjusting communication register mid-meeting. This is the skill that separates operators from executives.
> 3. **Procedural Reasoning** = **Playbook execution** — when the situation is chaotic, what is your documented escalation protocol? What steps are non-negotiable before you call an audible?
> 4. **Conditional Reasoning** = **Scenario planning and long-horizon thinking** — "If we don't solve this now, where is this business unit in 6 months?" Leaders who can't hold a future state under present pressure make reactive decisions.
> 5. **Pragmatic Reasoning** = **Constraint-aware execution** — you have a deadline, limited staff, and an unavailable stakeholder. What can actually happen today? Brilliant strategy that ignores resource reality is just fiction.
>
> Then: *"What is the equivalent of an OTA unilaterally revising a treatment plan — and what organizational governance structure exists to prevent that in a healthy company?"*

## 🔗 SIDE-BY-SIDE ANCHOR

| OTA Student Language | C-Level Executive Language |
|---|---|
| Narrative Reasoning (client's story) | Context intelligence — historical and cultural backstory before intervention |
| Interactive Reasoning (real-time adjustment) | Situational awareness — reading the room and adjusting in the moment |
| Procedural Reasoning (what's the next step) | Playbook execution — documented escalation protocol |
| Conditional Reasoning (where will this lead) | Scenario planning — long-horizon thinking under present pressure |
| Pragmatic Reasoning (what's actually possible) | Constraint-aware execution — resource-reality check |
| Report to supervising OT | Escalate to decision authority — never act unilaterally above your scope |
| Document observations without changing plan | Create a paper trail; preserve chain of command |
| Treatment plan revision (OT only) | Strategic pivot decision (C-Suite authority, not Director-level) |
