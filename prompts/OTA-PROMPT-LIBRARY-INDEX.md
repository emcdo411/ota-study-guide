## What This Is

Eight advanced study prompts — one per OTA domain — each structured as a **dual-lens learning system**:

- 🔵 **OTA Student Layer** — teaches the clinical domain from zero, using real patient scenarios, freshman-accessible language, and the domain's memorization framework as the spine
- 💼 **C-Level Executive Layer** — translates every clinical concept into business, leadership, and operations language for non-technical professionals supporting or funding healthcare teams
- 🔗 **Side-by-Side Anchor Table** — maps every clinical term to its executive equivalent so both audiences share a reference vocabulary
- 📌 **A&P Lab Final Image Connections** — links each domain to the anatomical images in your lab final study guide

Each prompt is designed to be dropped directly into a study session, a tutoring conversation, or a team training session and produce immediate, structured learning output.

## How to Use These Prompts

### For OTA Students
1. Read the **Domain prompt file** for the week you're studying
2. Answer the **OTA Student Study Prompt** in writing before looking at your notes — retrieval practice beats re-reading every time
3. Check your answer against your domain module
4. Complete the **Self-Assessment Checkpoint** at the end of each prompt
5. Use the **A&P Lab Final Image Connections** section to tie anatomy visuals to clinical function

### For Program Directors / Instructors
- Use the **C-Level Executive prompts** for community advisory board presentations
- Use the **Side-by-Side Anchor tables** to onboard non-clinical stakeholders (administration, HR, finance) into clinical quality conversations
- Use the **Bonus Practice Drills** (Domain 8) as formative assessment tools

### For Healthcare Leaders & Executives
- Start with the **C-Level Executive Translation Prompt** for each domain
- Use the **Side-by-Side Anchor table** as your translation dictionary
- The closing questions in each executive prompt are designed for board-level conversations about clinical quality, compliance, and operational risk

## A&P Lab Final: Core Image Categories

Your lab final study PDF contains images across these categories — each domain prompt connects to at least one:

- **Back musculature** (superficial + deep layers) → Domain 1, 2
- **Movement demonstrations** (supination/pronation, elevation/depression, dorsiflexion, inversion/eversion, opposition/reposition) → Domain 1, 3, 7
- **Bone microstructure** (osteon, haversian canal, compact vs. spongy bone) → Domain 4, 5, 6
- **Macroscopic bone anatomy** (femur, hand/carpals, skull, scapula, hip/coxal, thoracic vertebra, patella) → Domain 3, 7, 8
- **Skin anatomy** (epidermis, dermis, hypodermis, sensory receptors) → Domain 6, 8
- **Microscope parts** → Domain 4 (procedural hierarchy analogy)
