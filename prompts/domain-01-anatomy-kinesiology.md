# Domain 1 Prompt: Anatomy & Kinesiology
**Framework Trigger:** `ROMAM` | **Audience:** OTA Freshman + C-Level Executive (Non-Technical)

---

## 🔵 ADVANCED STUDY PROMPT

> You are a first-year OTA student who has never taken anatomy before. Your instructor just handed you a goniometer and told you to measure shoulder abduction on a patient. You have no idea where to start.
>
> Walk me through **Domain 1: Anatomy & Kinesiology** using the **ROMAM framework** — step by step — as if I am completely new to healthcare. For every technical term you use, explain it like I'm 18 years old and this is my first week of college.
>
> Then answer these questions in order:
> 1. What is **Range of Motion (ROM)** and why does it matter for a patient trying to button their shirt?
> 2. What is the difference between **Active ROM** and **Passive ROM** — and what does it tell you about a patient's muscle function?
> 3. Walk me through the **Manual Muscle Testing (MMT) scale** from 0 to 5. Give me a real-life picture for each number — what does a Grade 0 patient look like? A Grade 3? A Grade 5?
> 4. What are the three **planes of movement** and give me one everyday activity that happens in each plane?
> 5. What is **origin vs. insertion** — and why does it matter which end of the muscle moves?
>
> End with a checkpoint: *"If I can now explain MMT grades to a classmate without looking at my notes, what does that tell me about my readiness for the NBCOT exam?"*

## 💼 C-LEVEL EXECUTIVE TRANSLATION PROMPT

> You are a Chief Operating Officer. You have no medical background. You just hired a team of OTAs for your hospital system and your CFO is asking why clinical measurement tools matter for operational efficiency.
>
> Explain **Domain 1: Anatomy & Kinesiology** to me using only business, operations, and leadership language. No anatomy jargon unless you immediately translate it.
>
> Frame it this way:
> 1. **ROM (Range of Motion)** = What is the machine's operating range? How do we know if a unit is underperforming versus broken?
> 2. **Active vs. Passive ROM** = Who is doing the work — the employee or the manager? What does self-initiated output vs. assisted output tell you about team capacity?
> 3. **MMT Grades 0–5** = Think of this as your **performance rating scale**. What does a "Grade 0" employee on a critical project look like vs. a "Grade 5"? How does knowing that rating change your resource allocation?
> 4. **Planes of movement** = What are the **three dimensions of any workflow** — vertical (hierarchy), lateral (cross-functional), and rotational (pivot decisions)?
> 5. **Origin vs. Insertion** = Where does the authority anchor (the policy, the HQ) and where does the action land (the field team, the output)?
>
> Close with a leadership insight: *"If my clinical teams can't measure baseline function accurately, what downstream operational risks am I accepting as a COO?"*

## 🔗 SIDE-BY-SIDE ANCHOR

| OTA Student Language | C-Level Executive Language |
|---|---|
| Measuring ROM with a goniometer | Benchmarking operational capacity before intervention |
| MMT Grade 0 = no muscle contraction visible | Grade 0 performer = no output, no initiative, cannot self-start |
| Active ROM = client moves themselves | Self-directed output — employee owns the task |
| Passive ROM = therapist moves the limb | Manager-assisted output — dependency on leadership to execute |
| Origin = fixed attachment point | Policy anchor / headquarters authority |
| Insertion = moving end of muscle | Field team / execution layer |
| Sagittal plane (flexion/extension) | Vertical hierarchy decisions |
| Frontal plane (abduction/adduction) | Cross-functional lateral collaboration |
| Transverse plane (rotation) | Strategic pivot or rotation decisions |
