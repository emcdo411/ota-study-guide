# Domains 1 & 2: Anatomy & Kinesiology + Therapeutic Techniques

---

## DOMAIN 1: ANATOMY & KINESIOLOGY

### 🎓 [STUDENT] — Layer A: Study Module

**Core Concepts:**
- **Kinesiology**: The science of human movement, including biomechanics, muscle function, and joint mechanics
- **Range of Motion (ROM)**: The arc of movement at a joint; measured in degrees using a goniometer
- **Active ROM (AROM)**: Movement produced by the client's own muscle contraction
- **Passive ROM (PROM)**: Movement produced by the therapist without client muscle effort
- **Muscle grades (MMT)**: Manual Muscle Testing scale (0–5) assessing strength from no contraction (0) to normal strength against resistance (5)
- **Planes of movement**: Sagittal (flexion/extension), Frontal (abduction/adduction), Transverse (rotation)
- **Origin vs. Insertion**: Origin = proximal, fixed attachment; Insertion = distal, moving attachment

**Memorization Framework:**
**РОМАМ** — ROM Assessment Matters for All Movement
- **R**ange (measure it)
- **O**rigin/Insertion (know the muscle)
- **M**ovement plane (sagittal, frontal, transverse)
- **A**ctive vs. Passive (distinguish who moves it)
- **M**uscle grade (score it 0–5)

**High-Yield Exam Tips:**
- 🔵 [Multiple Choice] Know the 6 cardinal planes and the movements within each — NBCOT loves to ask which plane an ADL motion occurs in
- 🟠 [Case Scenario] When a scenario describes a client post-stroke with flaccid limb, identify correct MMT grade (0 or 1) and appropriate intervention approach
- 🟢 [Clinical Application] Practice goniometry positioning for shoulder, elbow, wrist, hip, knee, and ankle — these appear in clinical application questions as setup errors

**Self-Assessment Checkpoint:**
> ❓ A client presents with shoulder abduction to 85°. What plane is this motion in, what is the normal ROM, and what grade would you document if they achieved this against gravity with no resistance?

---

### 💼 [EXEC] — Layer B: Executive Translation

**Plain-Language Summary:**
This domain is about understanding how the human body moves — which muscles do which jobs, what the normal range of a joint's movement looks like, and how to measure when something is off. OTAs use this knowledge every time they work with a client to make sure they're helping, not harming.

**Business/Leadership Analogy:**
Think of this as **systems mapping and performance benchmarking**. Before a COO can optimize operations, they need to know how every component of the system is supposed to function, what its capacity limits are, and how to measure deviation from baseline. You can't improve what you haven't measured.

> 💡 Organizations that lack anatomical literacy in their clinical staff carry invisible liability — the same way a factory ignoring equipment tolerances accumulates risk before failure.

---

### 🔗 Layer C: Connection Bridge

> Just as a COO must know each operational unit's normal capacity before diagnosing performance gaps, an OTA must know each joint's normal ROM before identifying dysfunction — both disciplines require baseline mastery before intervention.

## DOMAIN 2: THERAPEUTIC TECHNIQUES & MODALITIES

### 🎓 [STUDENT] — Layer A: Study Module

**Core Concepts:**
- **Preparatory methods**: Techniques used before purposeful activity to prepare the body (e.g., heat, ice, TENS, therapeutic exercise)
- **Purposeful activity**: Client-directed tasks with a therapeutic goal embedded (e.g., cooking as a shoulder ROM exercise)
- **Occupation-based intervention**: Real tasks in real contexts that are meaningful to the client (e.g., grocery shopping, bathing)
- **PAMs (Physical Agent Modalities)**: Thermal, electrical, or mechanical agents (heat packs, ultrasound, e-stim); OTAs may use only with advanced training and state regulation
- **Activity grading**: Modifying task difficulty up or down to match client's current skill level
- **Activity adaptation**: Changing the environment, tools, or method to enable task completion
- **NDT (Neurodevelopmental Treatment)**: Technique for clients with neurological conditions focusing on inhibiting abnormal tone and facilitating normal movement patterns

**Memorization Framework:**
**PAPA-G** — The Intervention Hierarchy
- **P**reparatory methods (warm up the system)
- **A**ctivity — purposeful (goal-embedded task)
- **P**articipation in occupation (real life, real context)
- **A**daptation (change the task or environment)
- **G**rading (increase or decrease challenge)

**High-Yield Exam Tips:**
- 🔵 [Multiple Choice] Know the hierarchy: Preparatory → Purposeful → Occupation-based. Exams frequently ask which intervention type is MOST appropriate — occupation-based is usually highest value
- 🟠 [Case Scenario] If a scenario describes an OTA using ultrasound, check: Does the OTA have PAM certification? Is it within state law? Is it preparatory only?
- 🟢 [Clinical Application] Be ready to grade or adapt a cooking, dressing, or bathing activity for clients at different functional levels

**Self-Assessment Checkpoint:**
> ❓ Your client with hemiplegia wants to return to cooking. Design one preparatory technique, one purposeful activity, and one occupation-based intervention for this goal. What's the difference between grading and adapting for this client?

---

### 💼 [EXEC] — Layer B: Executive Translation

**Plain-Language Summary:**
This domain is about the actual tools and strategies OTAs use during treatment sessions — from physical warm-up techniques to real-world practice in meaningful activities. The goal is always to move the client from dependent to independent, using the most real-world-relevant activity possible.

**Business/Leadership Analogy:**
This maps directly to **change management and capability building**. You don't hand a struggling team a complex new system on Day 1 — you run readiness exercises (preparatory), move to simulated workflows (purposeful activity), then deploy in production (occupation-based). Grading the challenge is just calibrated rollout strategy.

> 💡 The most effective clinical interventions — like the most effective organizational change programs — meet people exactly where they are and progressively raise the bar.

---

### 🔗 Layer C: Connection Bridge

> The OTA's intervention hierarchy (preparatory → purposeful → occupation-based) mirrors an executive's change management playbook: condition the system first, simulate with purpose, then deploy in the real context — both approaches fail when leaders skip straight to full deployment without preparation.
