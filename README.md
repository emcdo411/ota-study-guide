## What This Is

A production-grade, dual-lens prompt library covering all eight NBCOT clinical domains for first-year Occupational Therapy Assistant students. Every domain is structured as two parallel learning tracks that run simultaneously:

- **🔵 OTA Student Layer** — clinical concepts taught from zero using real patient scenarios, freshman-accessible language, and a memorization framework as the structural spine
- **💼 C-Level Executive Layer** — every clinical concept translated into business, leadership, and operational language for non-clinical stakeholders supporting or funding OTA programs
- **🔗 Side-by-Side Anchor Table** — maps every clinical term to its executive equivalent so both audiences share a reference vocabulary
- **📌 A&P Lab Final Image Connections** — ties each domain to anatomical images in the lab final study guide

Each prompt is designed to be dropped directly into a study session, tutoring conversation, or team training context and produce immediate, structured learning output.

## How to Use This Library

### For OTA Students

1. Navigate to the `stress-tests/` folder and open the domain file for your current study week
2. Answer the **OTA Student Study Prompt** in writing before consulting your notes — retrieval practice beats re-reading every time
3. Check your answer against your course module
4. Use the **A&P Lab Final Image Connections** section to tie anatomy visuals to clinical function
5. Complete the self-assessment checkpoint at the end of each prompt

### For Program Directors & Instructors

- Use the **C-Level Executive prompts** for community advisory board presentations and stakeholder onboarding
- Use the **Side-by-Side Anchor tables** to bring non-clinical administrators (HR, finance, operations) into clinical quality conversations
- The **Bonus Practice Drills** in Domain 8 function as formative assessment tools for documentation competency

### For Healthcare Leaders & Executives

- Start with the **C-Level Executive Translation Prompt** for each domain
- Use the **Side-by-Side Anchor table** as your translation dictionary between clinical and operational language
- The closing questions in each executive prompt are engineered for board-level conversations about clinical quality, compliance, and revenue cycle risk

## The Dual-Lens Design Pattern

Every domain prompt is built on the same structural pattern:

```
Clinical Scenario (patient-centered, first-person, high-stakes)
    ↓
Framework Spine (memorization trigger for each element)
    ↓
OTA Student Walkthrough (freshman-accessible, step-by-step)
    ↓
C-Level Executive Translation (business/operations vocabulary)
    ↓
Side-by-Side Anchor Table (shared reference vocabulary)
    ↓
A&P Lab Final Connections (anatomy image → clinical function)
```

This pattern ensures the same intellectual content is accessible to an 18-year-old in their first clinical course and a Chief Revenue Officer reviewing a $2.3M denied claims report — simultaneously, without dumbing down either version.

## Sample: Domain 8 — SOAP Note Practice Drill

> *Setting: Outpatient OT clinic. Patient: 67-year-old female with Parkinson's disease, 3 months post-diagnosis. Session focus: meal preparation (making oatmeal on the stovetop). Goal: independent meal preparation within 8 weeks.*

Write the full SOAP note:

- **S**: What did she say about today's session and her goals?
- **O**: What did you observe and measure? (tremor severity, task steps completed independently, time on task, adaptive equipment used)
- **A**: What does today's data tell you about where she is relative to her goal?
- **P**: What is your plan for next session — and why does it require skilled OTA intervention?

Then write one **SMART-S goal** for her discharge plan.

*This drill mirrors the NBCOT documentation standard and is designed to surface documentation weakness before the exam does.*

## Sample: Domain 5 — Clinical Reasoning under Constraint

> *Your patient — a 45-year-old woman recovering from TBI — was making great progress last week. Today she walks in, sits down, crosses her arms, and says nothing.*

Apply all five NIPCP reasoning modes:

| Mode | Your Task |
|---|---|
| **Narrative** | What do you need to know about her life before the injury to understand today? |
| **Interactive** | She's not talking. What do you do in the next 60 seconds? |
| **Procedural** | What is your clinical protocol when a patient refuses to engage? |
| **Conditional** | If she stays disengaged for 3 more sessions, where does her trajectory go? |
| **Pragmatic** | You have 45 minutes and the OT is unavailable until 3pm. What is actually doable right now? |

## Executive Closing Questions (by Domain)

These questions are designed for board-level and leadership conversations. Each appears at the end of its respective C-Level Executive prompt.

| Domain | Closing Question |
|---|---|
| D1 | *"If my clinical teams can't measure baseline function accurately, what downstream operational risks am I accepting as a COO?"* |
| D2 | *"What is the organizational cost of a CEO who skips straight to full deployment without conditioning the workforce first?"* |
| D3 | *"If HR skips role clarity and task mapping before deploying performance improvement plans, what injury are they treating?"* |
| D4 | *"What is the institutional liability exposure when a frontline staff member acts beyond their credentialed authority?"* |
| D5 | *"What is the equivalent of an OTA unilaterally revising a treatment plan — and what governance structure prevents it?"* |
| D6 | *"What is the organizational equivalent of a therapist confusing sympathy with empathy?"* |
| D7 | *"What is the organizational cost of a CTO who defaults to maximum technology before auditing minimum viable alternatives?"* |
| D8 | *"If your documentation team cannot articulate why their service required specialized skill — in writing, in 3 sentences — what does that tell you about your revenue cycle vulnerability?"* |
